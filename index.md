I am Zirui Ren (任梓瑞), a third-year undergraduate at the Department of Physics, Tsinghua University. I am broadly interested in the emerging science of large models, especially the intersection of mechanistic interpretability, machine learning theory, and the science of AI.

I am currently advised by [Prof. Ziming Liu](https://kindxiaoming.github.io/) on physics of AI. Previously, I have also worked with [Prof. Zhiyuan Liu](https://nlp.csai.tsinghua.edu.cn/~lzy/) and collaborators at ModelBest on long-context language modeling.

## Research interests

I am particularly interested in:

- Mechanistic interpretability of LLMs and reasoning models
- Spectral and dynamical perspectives on neural network training
- Physics-inspired views of representation, dynamics, and generalization in AI

A recurring theme in my work is to combine **mechanistic analysis** with **toy models** and **controlled experiments** to uncover simple quantitative laws behind the behavior of complex models.

## Publications

**Are Your Reasoning Models Reasoning or Guessing? A Mechanistic Analysis of Hierarchical Reasoning Models**  
**Zirui Ren**, Ziming Liu  

_arXiv:2601.10679_. Under review at NeurIPS 2026.  

We perform a systematic mechanistic study of hierarchical reasoning models (HRMs) that achieve state-of-the-art performance on many reasoning tasks. By analyzing latent-state trajectories, we uncover violations of the fixed-point assumption and identify a hidden “guessing” behavior in HRMs. Building on these insights, we propose three augmentation techniques that improve HRM performance on Sudoku-Extreme from **55%** to **97%**, and release a complete open-source pipeline.

**LLM×MapReduce-V2: Entropy-Driven Convolutional Test-Time Scaling for Generating Long-Form Articles from Extremely Long Resources**  

Haoyu Wang, Yujia Fu, Zhu Zhang, Shuo Wang, **Zirui Ren**, Xiaorong Wang, Zhili Li, Chaoqun He, Bo An, Zhiyuan Liu, Maosong Sun  
_arXiv:2504.05732_. 

We develop an LLM test-time scaling framework inspired by convolutional architectures, enabling automated generation of **extremely long outputs from extremely long inputs**. Our method improves long-form coherence, structure, and reference utilization over strong baselines, and is deployed in an academic survey generation system for real-world use.
